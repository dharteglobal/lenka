About Lenka

I began my professional life as an artist. I studied fine art and graphic design, and worked in that creative world for years. The creativity never left me — it lives in every session I hold. But it was the turn from visual art to the body that transformed my path entirely.

My Training & Credentials

  • Biodynamic Craniosacral Therapy (2009–present) — trained at a London school under senior osteopaths and biodynamic facilitators. Completed additional specialist training in working with mothers and newborns.
  • Somatic Experiencing® (Peter Levine's trauma-release method) — certified practitioner in body-based trauma healing.
  • Hypnotherapy — trained in therapeutic hypnosis for anxiety, trauma, and emotional regulation.
  • Thai Massage — additional training in somatic bodywork.
  • Ayurvedic Wellness — retreat facilitation and holistic healing practices.

My Work

Since 2009, I've been specializing in nervous system healing and trauma recovery. My practice weaves craniosacral sessions into a broader framework that includes Somatic Experiencing®, hypnotherapy, and Thai massage. Clients come to me from across Central Europe and beyond — many after years on conventional treatment pathways. Some arrive simply curious. All, I believe, arrive because some part of them already senses that the body is trying to say something it has not yet been given space to say.

May–October: Prague-Based Practice

During these months, I see clients in person in my Prague practice, offering individual somatic sessions and craniosacral therapy. This is when you can experience the full depth of in-person healing work.

November–April: Retreats in India

During the winter months, I lead and organize Ayurvedic wellness retreats in India. These are spaces for deep rest, regeneration, and holistic healing — combining yoga, meditation, traditional therapies, and the healing power of time away. Retreats serve as both personal transformation and group healing experiences.

Workshops & Facilitation

I also facilitate healing workshops — including craniosacral therapy, Thai massage, somatic breath work, and Somatic Experiencing — which can be organized internationally for groups, corporates, or wellness centers.

What Drives My Work

My work is especially attuned to those navigating long-term or complex healing processes. I believe in encouraging personal responsibility while offering steady and compassionate guidance. I see my role not as "fixer," but as a guide — someone who creates safety so your own healing intelligence can do its work.

Every session is held by three things: trust, safety, and the quiet discipline of truly listening.
